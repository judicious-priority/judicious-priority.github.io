The Power to Exclude "Wastes of Time" (Rule 403) Under Federal Rule of Evidence 403 (and its state counterparts, such as Pennsylvania Rule of Evidence 403), a trial judge has the explicit authority to block completely relevant witness testimony if its delivery conflicts with the court's administrative efficiency. https://www.losangelescriminallawyer.pro/exclusion-of-relevant-evidence-in-criminal-trials.html • The Rule: The court may exclude relevant evidence if its value is substantially outweighed by a danger of undue delay, wasting time, or needlessly presenting cumulative evidence. • How it applies: If a party attempts to call three witnesses to testify to the exact same event, or if a judge determines a witness's testimony will take hours but only yield a tiny fraction of useful facts, the judge can block those witnesses from taking the stand entirely. *https://www.losangelescriminallawyer.pro/exclusion-of-relevant-evidence-in-criminal-trials.html 2.
